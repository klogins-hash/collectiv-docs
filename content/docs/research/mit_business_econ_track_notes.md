# MIT Business Economics Track - Key Details

## Program Structure
- **Joint program**: MIT Sloan Applied Economics Group + MIT Economics Department
- **Designed for**: Students who want to leverage rigorous economic analysis for complex business and policy issues
- **Cohort size**: 3-4 students per year (small, tight-knit community)
- **Duration**: 5-6 years

## Curriculum
- Same first-year coursework as standard Economics PhD
- Two major fields + one minor field in Economics Department
- **Two Applied Economics (AE) elective courses** (6-week modules):
  - Organizational Economics
  - Health Economics
  - Industrial Organization
  - Environmental Economics
  - Mechanism Design

## Dissertation Requirements
- **Must have TWO advisors**:
  1. One from Economics Department
  2. One from Applied Economics Group at MIT Sloan
- Original research contributing to business economics field

## Teaching/Research Requirements
### 5-year program:
- 2 terms as RA within Sloan
- 1 term as TA in Economics Department

### 6-year program:
- 2 terms as RA within Sloan
- 2 terms as TA in Economics
- 2 terms as TA for Sloan MBA/EMBA classes (important for future business school faculty)

## Job Market Preparation
- Seminars, workshops, mock interviews, presentation training
- Prepared for positions in:
  - Economics departments
  - Business schools
  - Policy schools
  - Research institutions
  - Government agencies

## Why This Matters for User
- **Explicitly designed for business applications** - not just pure theory
- **Dual advisor structure** means support from both academic economists and applied business faculty
- **Small cohort** = more flexibility and individual attention
- **Teaching MBA students** = experience with practitioner audience
- **"Real-world impact"** is explicitly stated goal
- **Applied Economics Group** focuses on using economic theory and econometrics for practical challenges

## Application Process
- Apply through standard Economics Department application
- Indicate interest in Business Economics track during application
- Admissions committee includes faculty from both Applied Economics Group and Economics Department
- **Important**: Expressing interest in Business Economics does NOT change likelihood of admission to standard Economics PhD

## Perfect Fit for User Because:
1. Explicitly bridges theory and practice
2. Small program = easier to negotiate unconventional research
3. Business school connection = more understanding of entrepreneurship
4. "Practical business applications" is core mission
5. Faculty collaboration across business and economics
6. Preparation for business school faculty positions (where practitioner experience is valued)
