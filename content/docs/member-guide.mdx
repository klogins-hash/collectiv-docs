## How Membership Works

**Step 1: Crowdfund Contribution**

Your ticket in is helping us crowdfund this thing. Your contribution goes into the general cooperative pool.

**This is not:**
- A monthly subscription
- A one-time purchase
- A donation

**This is:**
- An investment in cooperative equity
- A stake in everything we build
- Long-term wealth building

**Step 2: Onboarding**

Once your contribution is processed:
- You get access to all tools and resources
- You receive your equity stake
- You can participate in governance
- You can join working groups

**Step 3: Start Building**

What do you want to make? What can we help you build? Let's figure it out together.

**Where it goes:**

- Cooperative equity (ownership stake)

## What You Own

### Your IP (100%)

Everything you create is yours. Complete ownership. Full control.

**This means:**
- You decide what to do with your work
- You keep all revenue from your creations
- No platform takes a cut
- You can license, sell, or give away your work

**The cooperative never owns your IP.**

### Cooperative Equity

You own a stake in the cooperative itself. This includes:

**Ownership of:**
- The tools we build together
- The infrastructure we create
- The value the cooperative generates
- The pool capital and its growth

**Your equity grows when:**
- The cooperative pool grows
- Tools generate revenue
- Member projects create value
- New members join

**This is long-term wealth building.**

### How Equity Works

**Initial Stake:**
Your crowdfund contribution determines your initial equity stake.

**Equity Growth:**
As the cooperative grows, your equity value increases.

**Equity Valuation:**
We calculate equity value based on:
- Pool capital
- Revenue generated
- Tools and assets
- Member contributions
- Market comparables

**Equity Reporting:**
You can see your equity value anytime in your member dashboard.

## The Billionaire Trick

### Why You Won't Want to Cash Out

Your equity is growing. Why would you sell it and stop the growth?

**Billionaires don't cash out. They borrow.**

**Why?**
- Cashing out = taxes (30-40% gone)
- Borrowing = no taxes (you keep everything)
- Your equity keeps growing
- You get cash when you need it

**This is how real wealth is built.**

### How We Made It Easy

You don't need to figure out the complex financial stuff. We built it into the system.

**How it works:**

**Step 1:** Your equity grows over time

**Step 2:** When you need cash, you request a loan against your equity

**Step 3:** We handle all the financial infrastructure (we partner with lending providers)

**Step 4:** You get cash, no taxes, equity keeps growing

**Step 5:** You just swipe your card when you need money

**We handle the boring parts. You just build.**

### Borrowing Terms

**How much you can borrow:**
Typically 50-70% of your equity value (depends on lending partner terms)

**Interest rates:**
Competitive rates based on your equity value and market conditions

**Repayment:**
Flexible terms, or you can refinance as your equity grows

**The key insight:**
As long as your equity grows faster than the interest rate, you're building wealth.

## Governance & Participation

### Your Vote

You get one vote. Use it.

**What you vote on:**
- Tool development priorities
- Budget allocation
- Strategic decisions
- Governance changes
- Major partnerships

**How voting works:**
- Proposals posted to member forum
- Discussion period (7-14 days)
- Voting period (3-7 days)
- Results automatically calculated
- Implementation begins

**Minimum participation:**
Try to vote on at least the major decisions. Governance only works if members participate.

### Making Proposals

**Any member can propose anything.**

**Proposal process:**
1. Draft your idea (use the template)
2. Post to member forum
3. Discuss and refine
4. Call for vote
5. If approved, help implement

**Don't be shy.** If you have an idea, propose it.

## Financial Details

### Understanding Your Equity

**Your Dashboard Shows:**
- Current equity value
- Equity growth over time
- Pool capital status
- Revenue and distributions
- Borrowing capacity

**Equity Calculations:**
We use transparent formulas based on:
- Your contribution amount
- Time as a member
- Cooperative growth
- Pool capital value

**Updates:**
Equity valuations updated monthly.

### Revenue and Distributions

**How revenue flows:**

**Revenue sources:**
- Tool subscriptions (if we add external users)
- Service fees (if applicable)
- Member project success (voluntary contributions)
- Partnerships and integrations

**Revenue allocation:**
- Operations and development (60-70%)
- Member benefits and support (10-20%)
- Reserves and growth (10-20%)
- Distributions (if applicable, 0-10%)

**The goal:** Grow equity value, not extract cash.

### Tax Implications

**Your Contribution:**
May be treated as an investment (consult your tax advisor).

**Your Equity:**
Capital gains taxes only apply when you sell (if you sell).

**Borrowing:**
No taxes on borrowed money (that's the point).

**Your IP Revenue:**
You pay taxes on income you earn from your work (normal income tax).

**We're not tax advisors.** Consult a professional for your specific situation.

## FAQ

### Can I leave anytime?

Yes. You can resign your membership and settle your equity at current valuation.

### What if the cooperative fails?

Your IP is always yours. Equity value would be distributed according to cooperative bylaws.

### Can I sell my equity?

Potentially, but borrowing against it is usually smarter (no taxes, keeps growing).

### How is equity valued?

Based on pool capital, revenue, assets, and member contributions. Transparent formula, updated monthly.

### What if I disagree with a decision?

Vote against it. Propose an alternative. That's how democracy works.

### Can I contribute more later?

Yes. Additional contributions increase your equity stake.

### Do I have to participate in governance?

You should, but there's no strict requirement. We encourage at least voting on major decisions.

### Can I collaborate with other members?

Absolutely. That's encouraged.

### What if I need help with a project?

Ask in the forum or chat. Members often help each other.

### Is my data private?

Yes. We don't sell or share member data. See our privacy policy for details.
